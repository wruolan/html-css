Our reliance on vision is deeply rooted in biology. Consider these facts:

 40% of the brain’s nerve fibers connect to the retina.
50% of our neural tissue is involved in visual processing.
90% of the information our brain receives is visual.

Losing sight impacts more than just what we see—it reshapes how we interpret the world. This change is often met with fear and misunderstanding.

But for blind and visually impaired individuals, independence isn’t tied to seeing — it’s tied to information. Their brain’s adaptability allows the visual cortex to process non-visual information, like sound, touch, and spatial awareness, through which they navigate and understand the world.
