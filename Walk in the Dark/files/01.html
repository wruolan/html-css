Have you ever paused to think about how much independence you exercise in your daily life? Crossing a street, ordering a coffee, or preparing for a job interview—these are tasks most of us take for granted. Each action is a quiet assertion of your autonomy: the ability to navigate the world, make decisions, and take care of yourself without assistance.

Now imagine if these seemingly simple tasks suddenly became obstacles.

What if crossing the street meant relying entirely on sound to determine when it’s safe?

What if ordering a coffee meant facing a menu you couldn’t read?

What if your abilities in a job interview were overshadowed by assumptions about your limitations?

For many blind and visually impaired people, these challenges are part of everyday life. Yet, their autonomy is not defined by the ability to see, but by their access to information and their courage to adapt.
