Wake up

You wake up in your office, eyes flicking up
The patient log you don’t remember writing.

Nothing wants to be held, names vanish,
faces drift like whispers you think you once knew.

In one breath, you’re running down that street by the river,
the water’s fast and empty, and you’re alone there, finally.

No, you’re simply just tired, you need some rest,
have some ibuprofen for that insistent migraine.

But in the next, it’s a darkened room, your hands gloved in shadow,
a life waiting to start or finish, though they won’t tell you which.

Somewhere else, we’re in a café. You’re laughing,
but it doesn’t last, it never does.

And you, standing outside it all, try to pin down what you see,
naming each blur, each flicker. Was it real, or is that the trick?

You insist this must have meant something,
but you don’t ask what.
