notepad

The notepad sits at the corner of your desk, its once thick stack of pages reduced to a thin, pale skeleton. You’d torn out page after page over the past weeks, each one pressed into service for something forgettable—quick phone numbers, overdue bills, grocery lists scrawled in the margin between sessions. Now, only a few sheets remain, each one blank, clean, and untouched, almost out of place amid the clutter of your desk.






You run your fingers over the current page, feeling the faint ridge where the previous sheets were torn away. For a moment, you think of writing something down, if only to fill the empty space. But as your hand hovers over it, pen in hand, an odd sense of hesitation makes you stop.

It’s just paper. An empty page. But somehow, it feels as though it’s watching you, waiting for something important—something it knows you’ll eventually have to write down.
